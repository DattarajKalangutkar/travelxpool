Pernem Taluka

Pernem

Pernem taluka, the Northmost taluka of goa is surrounded by almost two most beautiful rivers of Goa. One is the Chapora river which is bounded at the south of Pernem taluka and the other is Tiracol River which is bounded entirely to the north side of Pernem Taluka. The distance between the Capital city of Goa which is Panjim to the Pernem taluka is almost around 27km.

As it is the northernmost taluka and its border is touched to the Maharashtra borders, it is said to be the entry gate for the Maharashtra people. If you visit the taluka, you will notice the people here, their culture, their living style, their occupation which is the same as the people in the olden and golden days of Goa.

As we are discussing or talking about the Pernem taluka, we cannot forget about the beautiful beaches, the historic places like temples, churches which give more beauty to the taluka.

Let's List down some Most beautiful beaches that Everyone should visit if you are in Goa and Specify in the Pernem Taluka.

  • Arambol Beach

    Arambol Beach is the one to spend time with family and budget outings.

  • Mandrem Beach

    Mandrem beach is situated at north goa in Pernem taluka, with the backdrop of mountain, one can view the sunset from it.

  • Udoo Beach

    Uddo beach is situated at the brink of chapora river with a view of chapora fort.

  • Ashwem Beach

    Ashvem beach is situated beside Morjim beach. Famous for its foreign tourist visits.

  • Ozran Beach

    Ozran beach is a small rocky beach situated behind the chapora fort.

Besides not only beaches but there are also others Places which attract the Taluka.

Here is the List of Historic Places you will love to see

  • Bhagwati Devi Temple

    This Bhagwati Devi Temple is of Goddess Parvati temple which is the most ancient Temple in Goa at around 500 years old and is situated near the main road.

  • Somnath Temple, Pernem

    This beautiful Temple which is situated at Rauraje Deshprabhu Royal Palace, Pedne, Pernem, Goa is a temple of Goddess Mahadev.

  • Kaalbhairav Temple

    This is a temple that needs to be visited during the rainy season mostly, as besides this temple there is a small river that flows beside the temple which calms the surrounding environment. There is all greenery around the place, the place is very quiet and cool for peace.

  • Shri Chandreshwar Bhoothnath Temple

    The most beautiful and oldest Temple which surround by beautiful nature present on the hilltop of Quepem.Chandreshwar is an incarnation of Lord Shiva which celebrates festive like five day Hanuman Jayanti, Dussehra, and Mahashivratri.

  • Deshprabhu House

    this is a famous monument in Pernem and also Goa's grandest Hindu Mansion which has another name as Sitaram Vilas and the Viscount's Palace. In this Place you can see a museum and Temple. in this museum, you the see picture of the first-ever car imported to Goa.

  • The Chapel

    The original Chapel in Pernem was built in 1852 and erected into a Parish on January 02, 1855. St. Joseph Church, Pernem was rebuilt in 1864. Fr. Camilo Simoes was appointed as the Parish Priest. The Parish was renovated in 2002. The Parish has 5 substations constituted of 11 Small Christian Communities.

  • Fort Tiracol

    This fort is present at the mouth of the River Tiracol, which can be visited through water transport such as a ferry from the Keri beach.

Now let us try to see some Facts about Pernem Taluka

  • The Festive is called Pednechi Punav – a unique way to celebrate the Dussehra in Goa from the rest of the talukas.

  • One needs to climb steps of around 120 from the parking area to reach the Shri Chandreshwar Bhoothnath Temple.

  • In the Deshprabhu House, there are about 14,000 soldiers with cavalry and elephants.

Every Goa taluka has a market day and Pernem taluka has Thursday as a market day. here people over all the taluka as well people outside or nearby taluka come to buy fruits and vegetables. it is said that Pernem taluka market has a cheap rate for every product they buy, whether it is fruits or vegetables or some other stuff.
